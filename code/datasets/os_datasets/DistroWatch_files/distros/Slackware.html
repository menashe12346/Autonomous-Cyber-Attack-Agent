The Official Release of Slackware Linux by Patrick Volkerding is an advanced Linux operating system, designed with the twin goals of ease of use and stability as top priorities. Including the latest popular software while retaining a sense of tradition, providing simplicity and ease of use alongside flexibility and power, Slackware brings the best of all worlds to the table. Originally developed by Linus Torvalds in 1991, the UNIX-like Linux operating system now benefits from the contributions of millions of users and developers around the world. Slackware Linux provides new and experienced users alike with a fully-featured system, equipped to serve in any capacity from desktop workstation to machine-room server. Web, ftp, and email servers are ready to go out of the box, as are a wide selection of popular desktop environments. A full range of development tools, editors, and current libraries is included for users who wish to develop or compile additional software.

 I've kept a Slackware install for what may be decades now. Never a daily driver, more of a curiosity, in addition to being the one distro where I let it install the whole schmeer, which means tons of weirdly specific KDE educational apps for one thing. Care to learn Japanese or the periodic table? It's all there!

Seriously though, Slackware is the grandaddy of them all and it shows. Fiercely adhering to a philosophy that embraces lack of automated dependency checking is something no other distro could get away with, but with Slackware it's kinda charmingly retro. Much respect to Patrick and his helpers across the globe for doggedly keeping Slackware up to date. If you're on -current it's actually very much up to date aside from KDE (so far - I expect Plasma 6 will be there eventually).

I hope this doesn't come off as negative. I love Slackware! The Linux landscape would look much different if not for this very unique, seminal distro that keeps doing what it does, trends be damned.

 I'm running Slackware since years 2000s, actually using Slackware 15.0 on iMac and two laptops.

It's the best Linux distro I've ever seen, stable, fast, highly personalizable and fully transparent to users and administrators.
Of course it isn't a Linux version for newbies, anyway its the best choice to fully understand the Linux core system and create an OS personalized on your needs.

I like lightweight Desktop environments and Fluxbox is the best choice ... why ? You can setup everything you need, nothing more, nothing less. XFE as filmanager is also a good choice.
Using rsync for backups with personalized bash script even if there's a lot of backup tools also with GUI interface.
Software updates made by running pesonalized Cron scheduled scripts and ftps from Slackware software mirros.
Software packages built from SBO (SlackBuilds) or compiled from sources.
Servers running fast and with security in mind enforced by personal configurations.

In few words: Linux Slackware simply works fine and it's a wonderful computer experience.

Cheers.
